History - 1960's

1960 Rise of the Compacts

The 1960's saw a demand for smaller more compact cars. The Volkswagen bug and other foreign models were gaining popularity in the US. Due to this demand the big three US auto companies answered the call. In 1960 Ford introduced the Falcon, Chevy introduced the Corvair, and Plymouth introduced the Valiant. All small, lightweight 6-cylinder cars.

1964 The Muscle Era Begins

Although compacts were popular in the early 60s, the public had a demand for speed. This was answered by the muscle cars and pony cars. Muscle cars were mid-sized cars such as the Pontiac GTO, Chevy Chevelle, Ford Galaxy, and Dodge Charger. Pony cars were smaller compact cars like the Ford Mustang, Plymouth Barracuda, and Chevy Camaro. The idea was to put the biggest engine possible into these small and mid-sized cars to make them go extremely fast.
