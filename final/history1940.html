History - 1940's

Short Lived Success

The 1930s saw many luxuries become standard. Electric starters, synchromesh transmissions, automatic chokes, and Ford came out with the flathead V8. The 40’s started out on a high note for car innovation. This however was short lived. The world was at war and car production needed to switch to tank, aircraft, and weapons production.

No Cars for the Public

By 1943 no cars were being produced at all for the general public. The big car makers were still in business though. Ford was building the B24 Liberator, jeeps, and tank engines. GM built aircraft engines, guns, ammo, and military trucks. Chrysler built air raid sirens and the B-29 engines. All manufacturing efforts were being made to the war.

Rise Again

After the success of winning the war, car manufacturing started up again. We even saw some new manufacturers on the block. The big three US car makers started up production and the first batch of cars were still basically 30’s era at first. Preston Tucker introduced his car in 1948 and it was so advanced and innovative that it scared the major companies into making some changes. It came with seatbelts, rear engine, three headlights, and numerous safety features. Unfortunately, he was ran out of business but, he made a huge impact on the automotive industry. The 50’s were about to see a major boom (in cars as well as babies)
