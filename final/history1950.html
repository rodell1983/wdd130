History - 1950's

Golden Era

The 50’s saw a huge boom in cars, both with sales and innovation. American soldiers had returned from the war, moved to the suburbs and started their families. This meant having a car was essential. The car companies were ready to meet demand and they had a few tricks up their sleeves. A new concept for the 50’s became standard. Car companies started changing the car design every year to keep Americans wanting the latest model. This lasted throughout the decade into the 60s.

Hot Rods and the Need for Speed

Another phenomenon happened as a result from all those young men returning from action. They had a need for speed. In the 1950’s the hotrod was introduced. This meant take as much weight and unnecessary metal off the car and put the biggest engine you can in it (Sounds like a prelude to the muscle car) Car makers answered the call with the Corvette and the Thunderbird, although neither technically considered hotrods.

Bigger is Better

In 1955 chevy introduce the first car in one of the most iconic series of cars. As mentioned earlier, the car makers came up with the standard of changing car models every year. This caused them to have to “one up” themselves each time. As a result, cars were getting bigger and flashier each year. This patten can be seen between 1955 -1957 in chevy cars; they were better each year. However, by 1958 car makers had resorted to adding bigger tailfins and more headlight and the cars had just become pricier and more bloated. Americans will soon want to ditch these giant cars for something smaller and more economical.
