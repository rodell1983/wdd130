History 1970-1979

Death of the Muscle Car

1970 saw the peak of the muscle car era however it was about to be killed. Not only were safety mandates changing cars performance, but emissions were also being regulated. In 1973 all cars were required to have crash rated front and rear bumpers, so the cool stylish chrome bumpers were all replaced with large and bulky bumpers that could withstand a collision. In 1974 all cars were required to be equipped with catalytic converters and gasoline was required to be unleaded. This cause performance to hit an all-time low.

Oil Embargo

Just when the auto industry though it couldn’t get any worse the oil embargo hit in the mid 70’s. Although the performance of cars was severely hurt by emissions regulation, most cars were still gas hogs. During this time gasoline was hard to come by. People had to wait in long lines and were limited to how much fuel they could buy. This was the nail in the coffin to muscle cars and the birth of the subcompact and foreign invasion.

Sub Compacts and Imports

Starting in the early 70s the writing was on the wall. American car makers had to do something for the upcoming emissions and fuel shortages. Fords answer was the pinto and Chevys was the Vega. By the mid 70’s AMC came out with the pacer. This was an attempt to answer the emissions crackdown, fuel shortage, and a new phenomenon that was starting. Americans were starting to buy foreign cars. Car buyers wanted smaller and more economical cars and the new American subcompact were not cutting it. Buy the end of the 70s the amount of foreign (specifically) Asian cars had exploded. Stay tune for the 80’s where we’ll discuss the Asian invasion.
